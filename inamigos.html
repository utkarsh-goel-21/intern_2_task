Introduction

An NGO built around practical, community-led change.

Founded on September 23, 2020 by Mr. Govind Shukla, InAmigos Foundation works to bridge gaps in education, healthcare, economic opportunity, and social inclusion. The foundation brings volunteers, local communities, and partners together to create sustainable solutions for people who need support most.

Its work is backed by public credentials including Section 8 registration, 80G and 12A certifications, CSR-1 registration, NITI Aayog recognition, and ISO 9001:2015 certification.

Ongoing Projects

Six initiatives, one shared purpose.

Each program focuses on a specific social need while supporting the broader goal of stronger, more inclusive communities.

Project SEVA

Food, clothing, and basic relief

Supports underprivileged families through meal distribution, clothing drives, winter relief, and street-level service that protects dignity during hardship.

Project BACHPANSHALA

Learning support for children

Bridges education gaps through basic school support, digital literacy, life skills, learning sessions, and access to study material.

Project JEEV

Care for stray and abandoned animals

Feeds and supports stray animals while encouraging humane treatment, rescue awareness, and everyday compassion for vulnerable lives.

Project UDAAN

Women empowerment and self-reliance

Works with rural self-help groups and communities to promote skill development, financial independence, confidence, and menstrual hygiene awareness.

Project PRAKRITI

Environmental sustainability

Encourages tree plantation, conservation awareness, sustainable living, waste responsibility, and greener community habits.

Project VIKAS

Skills, internships, and youth growth

Creates internships and volunteer opportunities in fields such as research, content writing, social work, finance, marketing, and operations.

Social Impact

The purpose is long-term empowerment, not temporary visibility.

Access

Education programs and skill-building opportunities help children and young people move toward confidence, employability, and independence.

Dignity

Food, clothing, and relief drives address immediate needs while reminding communities that care must include respect.

Equity

Women-focused initiatives support financial participation, health awareness, and stronger decision-making power in families and communities.

Sustainability

Environmental and animal welfare work expands the idea of service to include shared spaces, local ecology, and all living beings.
